Learning objectives

Learning objectives

In this lesson, students will learn to:

  • analyse the impact of different factors on where a business chooses to locate itself
  • explain the link between what a business does and where it is located.

Key definitions

Subject vocabulary

  • brownfield sites: areas of land that were once used for urban development
  • competitors: rival businesses who sell a similar good or service
  • greenfield sites: previously undeveloped areas of land, usually on the outskirts of towns and cities
  • labour: people employed in a business/used in production
  • market: a place where buyers and sellers come together to exchange goods and services
  • materials: raw ingredients or parts used in producing a finished good or service
  • primary sector: extracting raw materials from the earth
  • secondary sector: converting raw materials into finished or semi-finished goods
  • tertiary sector: selling goods to a consumer or providing services

 

Key definitions: general vocabulary

  • relocated: moved to another place
  • congestion: problems caused by too much traffic in one place
  • proximity: a measure of how close one thing is to another

Factors affecting business location: proximity to market


Where might the following businesses locate themselves if they want to be close to their markets?

  • Sandwich shop
  • Hairdresser
  • Gardener
  • Producer of car windscreens

Factors affecting business location: proximity to market

  • Close to where the end consumer lives, shops or works
  • Close to where business customers are located in order to reduce transport costs
  • Close to transport links so consumers can easily access/park
  • Close to similar businesses (competitors) so customers know where to visit
  • On busy streets to catch passing trade
  • Close to businesses selling linked goods (for example, souvenir stalls close to tourist attractions)

Student work 1

Questions 1 to 3

  1. The Renault case study on p. 58 of the Student Book lists six factors that might have attracted Renault to Morocco.
    Rank the factors in order of their importance in Renault’s decision to locate in Tangier.
  2. Explain your answer to Question 1 to a partner. Discuss reasons for any different opinions.
  3. Write three sentences which explain the factors Renault may have considered in relation to the supply of labour when making its location decision. (The supply of labour refers to how easily a business can find and employ suitable workers.)

Factors Renault may have considered in relation to the supply of labour when making its location decision could include:

  • The degree of experience or skills in car manufacturing that workers in Tangier have or how much it would cost to train them.
  • What the levels of wages are for workers in Tangier and how this compares to other locations.
  • How many potential workers live in the area and whether they could travel to the factory each day for work.

Teaching session 2

Materials, competitors and location

A styled town map

Factors affecting business location: proximity to materials

  • Being close to materials reduces transport costs.
  • This is particularly important for large or heavy materials.
  • Brownfield sites are urban areas which have been previously built on but are not used any longer.
  • They are attractive to companies as they offer cheap rent and are likely to be close to labour or transport.
  • Greenfield sites have never been built on but may be cheap as they are in rural locations, however they may not be close to customers/labour.

End-of-lesson activity

Questions 5 to 7

5 Select one correct answer.

Which of the following factors is likely to be most important when choosing a location for a hairdresser business?

  1. Proximity to materials
  2. Close to businesses involved in manufacturing
  3. Access to cheap energy supplies
  4. Close to good parking and bus links

Answer

D: Close to good parking and bus links

6 Select one correct answer.

Which of the following businesses is most likely to locate itself on a high street in a busy residential area?

  1. A farm growing wheat and barley
  2. A real estate agent
  3. A factory producing cars
  4. A warehouse for an online retailer

Answer

B: A real estate agent

7 Select one correct answer.

A shop selling school uniform and equipment has decided to relocate its business. Which one of the following is the most likely reason for this move?

  1. For better access to materials
  2. To be closer to a wide range of skilled workers
  3. To move closer to the school and customers
  4. To be further away from competitors

Answer

C: To move closer to the school and customers
