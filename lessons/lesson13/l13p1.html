Lesson 13 | Overview Factors that Determine the Location of a Business 1

1.4.1
The main factors influencing location decisions and relocation of a business:
  • proximity to market, labour, materials and competitors
  • nature of the business activity.

In this lesson, students will learn to:

  • analyse the impact of different factors on where a business chooses to locate itself
  • explain the link between what a business does and where it is located.

Students should already be familiar with the classification of businesses into primary, secondary and tertiary activities (see Lesson 11).

Subject vocabulary

  • brownfield sites: areas of land that were once used for urban development
  • competitors: rival businesses who sell a similar good or service
  • greenfield sites: previously undeveloped areas of land, usually on the outskirts of towns and cities
  • labour: people employed in a business/used in production
  • market: a place where buyers and sellers come together to exchange goods and services
  • materials: raw ingredients or parts used in producing a finished good or service
  • primary sector: extracting raw materials from the earth
  • secondary sector: converting raw materials into finished or semi-finished goods
  • tertiary sector: selling goods to a consumer or providing services

General vocabulary

  • relocated: moved to another place
  • congestion: problems caused by too much traffic in one place
  • proximity: a measure of how close one thing is to another

  • Students sometimes think that online businesses do not have any physical base at all. This tends to be because they are simply thinking of the point of interaction with customers and seeing a virtual sales interface as being the business’s only ‘location’. You could explain that online businesses may still require physical premises including production facilities, warehousing, delivery infrastructure or offices for functions such as marketing and finance, and that even the owners of small enterprises with none of the above will still operate the business from a physical location, even if this is their home.
  • Some students may also think that businesses do not make an active decision about where to locate and instead just find a location by chance. Explain that, for many businesses, location is an important strategic decision.
