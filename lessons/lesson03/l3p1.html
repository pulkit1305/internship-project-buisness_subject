Lesson 3 | Overview Why Aims and Objectives Change

1.1.2
Why business aims and objectives change as businesses evolve: in response to market conditions, technology, performance, legislation and internal reasons.

In this lesson, students will learn to understand the different reasons why a business may change its objectives.

Students should already be familiar with:

  • the different types of financial (see Lesson 1) and non-financial (see Lesson 2) objectives that a business may set
  • reasons why those objectives may have been chosen in the first place.

Subject vocabulary

  • automation: use of machines instead of people to do a job
  • economies of scale: financial advantages of producing something in very large quantities
  • legislation: law or set of laws.

General vocabulary

  • evolve: to develop and change gradually over a long period of time

  • Some students may not appreciate that businesses operate in a dynamic environment and so changing objectives is the norm, rather than the exception. Use examples of changes that they will be familiar with (such as changes in technology) to show this as a natural process. For example, sales targets changing for producers of phones as the technology has moved from fixed line cradle-style handsets, ‘brick’ mobiles of the 1980s, WAP (Wireless Application Protocol) phones in the early 2000s, the first smartphones and now a modern smartphone with opportunities to sell apps, music, in-game adverts and top-ups etc.
  • Some students may expect a certain influencing factor to always have the same impact on a business’s objectives. Explain that there are lots of different variables which influence objectives and so an external factor (that will affect all businesses in an industry) may cause one to change its objectives whilst another of its competitors, exposed to the same factor, may not change objectives.
