Learning objectives

Learning objectives

In this lesson, students will learn to understand the different reasons why a business may change its objectives.

Key definitions

Subject vocabulary

  • automation: use of machines instead of people to do a job
  • economies of scale: financial advantages of producing something in very large quantities
  • legislation: law or set of laws.

Key definitions

General vocabulary

  • evolve: to develop and change gradually over a long period of time

challenge, non-financial, increasing, owner, sales, independence and control, environment, financial, share, social, motivations

Some objectives are financial such as increasing the amount of profit that businesses make. Further examples of this type of objective might relate to sales, market share or be more specific to the personal monetary needs of the owner (e.g. providing a large enough income to fund an expensive hobby.) Another category of objectives are non-financial ones which do not tend to relate directly to money. Examples include social objectives such as improving the lives of workers or other members of the community or protecting the environment. Objectives that relate to personal motivations might also include providing a challenge, achieving personal goals or being able to exercise independence and control over a business.
