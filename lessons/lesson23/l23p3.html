Learning objectives

Learning objectives

In this lesson, students will learn to:

  • understand the impact on businesses of joining/leaving trading blocs
  • understand the impact of tariffs and the removal of tariffs on businesses.

Key definitions

Subject vocabulary

  • protectionism: use of trade barriers to protect domestic producers
  • quota: a physical limit on the amount of a good allowed into a country
  • tariff: a tax on imports to make them more expensive
  • trade barriers: measures used to restrict trade
  • trade bloc: a group of countries that join together and agree to reduce or eliminate the barriers to trade between themselves

Key definitions

General vocabulary

  • conjunction: a word used to connect clauses or sentences

Teaching session 1

The impacts on business of joining or leaving a trade bloc

What is a trade bloc?

Trade blocs promote closer economic ties between member nations by aligning (bringing together) laws and removing trade barriers. They usually have a common set of rules that create barriers to trade with businesses and consumers from outside of the bloc.

ACDG

Trade bloc features

·         Single shared currency

·         Common economic policies

·         Product standard alignment

·         Commonly agreed laws in certain areas

·         Removal of border checks

·         Free movement of workers, capital and goods

 

·         Removed or reduced trade barriers

 

Barriers to international trade:

  • Border checks and controls
  • Tariffs
  • Quotas
  • Administrative barriers such as strict product quality checks
  • Subsidies paid to domestic businesses

Question 2

2 Which piece of business legislation do you think should be removed to help businesses and why?

Tips

Think about:

  • areas of legislation: environmental, consumer or employee protection
  • specific legislation that is holding businesses back and how
  • why a particular law’s removal might help certain businesses

These will be country-specific. For example:

  • In France, businesses have to give customers a paper receipt for any transaction so that buying a €1 coffee or a newspaper means businesses have to spend time printing and then there is additional paper waste created because the consumer often does not want the receipt.
  • It is reported that in Belgium, employees can apply to leave their job for a year to travel the world. They are entitled to an allowance and have their job kept open for their return. This means businesses have to hire and train additional workers whom they may then need to find alternative work for when the worker returns.
