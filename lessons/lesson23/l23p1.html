In this lesson, students will learn to:

  • understand the impact on businesses of joining/leaving trading blocs
  • understand the impact of tariffs and the removal of tariffs on businesses.

Students should already be familiar with:

  • tariffs and (to some extent) how they work (see Lessons 14 and 21)
  • a trade bloc (see Lesson 14).

Subject vocabulary

  • protectionism: use of trade barriers to protect domestic producers
  • quota: a physical limit on the amount of a good allowed into a country
  • tariff: a tax on imports to make them more expensive
  • trade barriers: measures used to restrict trade
  • trade bloc: a group of countries that join together and agree to reduce or eliminate the barriers to trade between themselves

General vocabulary

  • conjunction: a word used to connect clauses or sentences

  • Some students may not appreciate that there is no universal model for how a trade bloc operates. The different levels of economic integration that blocs can choose are not something that will be examined. However, the fact that there are varying degrees of integration should be stressed during Teaching session 1. Reassure students that they are not expected to know the extent to which any particular trade bloc is integrated and any potentially relevant impacts that they identify or explain will be rewarded.
