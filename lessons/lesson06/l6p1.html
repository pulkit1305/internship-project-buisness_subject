Lesson 6 | Overview Private Limited Companies

1.2.1
The main types of business ownership: limited companies (private).
1.2.2
Characteristics according to size:
  • concepts of risk, ownership and limited liability
  • ownership, control, sources of finance, shareholders
  • appropriateness of different forms of ownership.

In this lesson, students will learn to understand the key features and the (dis)advantages of private limited company status.

Students should already be familiar with:

  • the concept of limited liability (see Lesson 5)
  • the difference between incorporated and unincorporated companies (see Lesson 5).
  • limited companies: business organisations that have a separate legal identity to their owners
  • limited liability: shareholders’ losses are limited to the amount of capital they have invested in the business
  • private limited company: a limited company in which shares cannot be sold to the general public and share ownership can be restricted
  • public limited company: a limited company in which shares can be sold to the general public and traded on a stock exchange
  • shareholders: the owners of limited companies

In this lesson, sticky notes are required.

  • Some students may be confused by the fact that a private limited company is abbreviated by ltd while a public limited company (the topic of Lesson 7) is abbreviated by plc. This is because plc could easily stand for private limited company too, therefore, this confusion is understandable but it is a really important distinction to recognise and remember. A useful mnemonic may be that ‘private’ contains a ‘t’ and so they should remember that ‘ltd’ (containing the ‘t’) is the correct abbreviation.
