Learning objectives

Learning objectives

In this lesson, students will learn to understand the key features and the (dis)advantages of private limited company status.

Key definitions

Key definitions

  • limited companies: business organisations that have a separate legal identity to their owners
  • limited liability: shareholders’ losses are limited to the amount of capital they have invested in the business
  • private limited company: a limited company in which shares cannot be sold to the general public and share ownership can be restricted
  • public limited company: a limited company in which shares can be sold to the general public and traded on a stock exchange
  • shareholders: the owners of limited companies

Teaching session 1

The features of a private limited company

Limited liability companies

  • Limited liability companies have unlimited liability
  • This means the owners are not responsible for the debts of the company
  • The owners have a separate legal identity to the business

Private limited companies – key features

  • Referred to as ‘limited' – usually shortened to ltd
  • Owned by one or more shareholders
  • Shareholders make all the important decisions
  • In small ltds shareholders manage the business on a day-to-day basis

Private limited companies – key information

  • Owners can sell shares in the company to help raise money to start or grow the company
  • Shareholders (owners) choose directors to run the company for them, but can do the role themselves
  • Private limited companies must pay corporation tax on the profits they earn
  • Private limited companies must complete a formal set-up process when they become incorporated

Forming a private limited company

The documents that need to be completed are:

  • Memorandum of Association: Provides information about the business and what its main activities will be
  • Articles of Association: Contains information about how the company will be run (voting rights etc.)

Student work 1

Task 1

Task 1: Prepare a ‘sales pitch’ for your business.

Cover all of the following points, plus any other details you think will be persuasive. Remember to keep your text concise.

  • Business name, overview of what the business does and the current form of ownership
  • Three key initial objectives of the business
  • What your product is and who you will sell it to (for example, school students, families, businesses which need IT services, etc.)
  • Three key reasons why your business is a good investment opportunity for new shareholders
  • How much total investment you are seeking and how many new shareholders you would ideally want
  • How much investors are likely to receive back and why you think you can promise this amount.

An example sales pitch:

  • Top Tips - an ‘up-market’ nail and manicure salon, providing those services to a wealthy set of customers in the business district. A sole trader looking to become a ltd.
  • Three key initial objectives of the business: Attract £60,000 of initial investment before launch, make a profit of £150,000 in the first year, expand sales by opening five more salons within 3 years
  • Top Tips provides nailcare, extensions and manicure treatments to wealthy businesspeople who want to be pampered in their lunch hour or by appointment to visit their offices
  • This is a unique investment opportunity because we target high-earners specifically, offer a ‘home-visit’ service as well and all technicians are fully qualified
  • New investors are sought to help finance the initial launch of the business and then to expand it quickly into the two other largest cities in the country. Investors will become shareholders, entitled to equal shares of the profits with the current and new owners
  • The figures in the objectives above are part of the company’s business plan which has convinced a major bank to lend us £50,000 if we can attract £90,000 of additional investment
  • We are seeking up to five new investors to invest £20,000 each. This would entitle each new owner to a 5% share of the business and if we hit our profit target for year 1 they would immediately have received 37.5% of their initial investment back by this point.

Task 2

Task 2: Your elevator pitch

  1. You have £20,000 to invest.
  2. You need to decide if you want to invest in any of the companies whose sales pitch is on the board. You may not touch the board or the sticky notes.
  3. You will only have 1 minute to view the investment opportunities so you may need to scan the pitches rather than read them fully.
  4. You may invest in two companies but will need to split your £20,000 between them.
  5. Before you view the sales pitches: write down any questions you want to ask the owners of the companies to help you decide whether to invest.
  6. After you have decided which company to invest in: Update your questions based on the specific information you now have about the companies you have chosen.

Example questions to ask the owners of the company/companies identified for potential investment:

  • What percentage ownership of the company will I get if I invest my full £20,000?
  • How many other owners will there be?
  • Will I be a director of the company?
  • How will decisions be taken?
  • How will we resolve any disagreements?
  • Who will be the chairperson of the company?
  • How much money will the owner(s) put into setting up the company?
  • Do all owners have equal voting rights if we find it difficult to make certain decisions?
  • What percentage of the total profits will I be entitled to? Why?

Teaching session 2

Advantages and disadvantages of private limited companies

Advantages of being a private limited company

  • Limited liability – can only lose what you invest
  • More owners to provide capital (finance)
  • Owners can control who buys shares in the company
  • Still relatively easy to set up (compared to the biggest companies)

Disadvantages of being a private limited company

  • Some cost involved in setting up
  • More owners to share profits between
  • Can be difficult to find new shareholders
  • Have to make certain information public about the company
