Learning objectives

Learning objectives

In this lesson, students will learn to:

  • understand the main features of a franchised business
  • examine the advantages and disadvantages of operating a franchise.

Key definitions

Key definitions

  • franchise:an arrangement where a business (the franchisor) allows another (the franchisee) to trade under their name and use their business systems
  • franchise fee:the cost of buying a franchise
  • franchisee:a business that buys the right to trade under the name of another business and to use the business system of that other business
  • franchisor: a business that sells the right to another business to trade under their name and to use their business system
  • multinational company: a large business with significant service or production operations in at least two countries
  • royalty payments: ongoing payments from the franchisee.

Teaching session 1

Franchises

Franchises

A franchise is a business arrangement where a large business (the franchisor) allows another smaller business (the franchisee) to trade under their name and use their business systems.

What types of resources or support would you like to receive from a franchisor if you were considering opening up a branch of a particular fast-food restaurant?

Answers

● Rights to use their name, logos etc.

● Start-up advice about running a small business

● Access to systems to help run the business (e.g. equipment, stock ordering system)

● Access to buy the machinery required to run the business

● Training in how to operate a franchise and train staff etc.

● All marketing, i.e. the franchisor will run adverts

● Exclusive access to a certain, pre-agreed geographical area

● Materials and equipment (e.g. uniforms)

  

What does a franchisee agree to provide to the franchisor?

Answers

  • Commitment to run the business as instructed by the franchisor
  • Pay a one-off start-up fee
  • Pay an ongoing fee (usually a percentage of sales)
  • Contribute to the cost of advertising

Franchising is an agreement that grants a small, new business the right to use the brand name and business systems of another, larger business anywhere they want. 

True or False?

Answer

False

Franchising is a popular way for entrepreneurs to run their own business because they get expert support, can use a famous brand name and make all the business decisions themselves.

True or False?

Answer

False
