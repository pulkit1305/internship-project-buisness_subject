Learning objectives

Learning objectives

In this lesson, students will learn to:

  • understand the opportunities globalisation creates for businesses
  • understand the threats globalisation creates for businesses.

Key definitions

Key definitions: Subject vocabulary

  • competitive advantage: being able to gain a favourable position against rival companies
  • hostile takeover: a company being taken over by another when it does not want to be
  • stakeholder: an individual or group with an interest in the operation of a business

 

Key definitions: General vocabulary

  • auction site: online website where products are sold to the person who offers the most money for them
  • migration: the movement of people from one country to another

Opportunities of globalisation for businesses

  • Access to larger markets
  • Lower average cost of producing or marketing each product
  • Access to a wider range and quantity of labour
  • Opportunities to reduce the amount of tax paid

Some possible businesses outside the communications sector to benefit from smartphones could be:

  • Farmers in remote areas being able to check prices at market without having to travel
  • Small retailers being able to sell internationally because consumers can access their products online
  • Traders in countries with high levels of corruption can make payments online without needing to pay bribes to officials, reducing their costs and increasing business activity
  • Businesses provide online training to workers, increasing their skills and productivity
  • Computer game design companies can create mobile games of apps to access a completely new market

Teaching session 2

Threats of globalisation

The threats of globalisation for businesses

  • Increased competition from global brands offering lower prices or heavily advertised goods

The threats of globalisation for businesses

  • International takeovers:

For example:

  • Multinationals taking over local companies will probably use their existing suppliers, reducing the demand for the companies who were previously supplying the companies.
  • The multinationals often install expensive machinery to replace workers and so this can lead to increasing unemployment in the domestic economy.

The threats of globalisation for businesses

  • Increased risk of external shocks:

Expand

For example, small companies are increasingly affected by events in countries on the other side of the world because globalisation has increased our reliance on materials, workers, products and services from other countries.
