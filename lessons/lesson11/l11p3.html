Learning objectives

Learning objectives

In this lesson, students will learn to:

  • distinguish between the three main sectors of the economy
  • understand the changes that have occurred in sectors over time.

Key definitions

Subject vocabulary

  • capital intensive: production activity that is dominated by machinery rather than by using human labour
  • chain of production: the stages involved in turning raw materials into finished goods for sale to consumers
  • de-industrialisation: an economy moving away from manufacturing activity, usually towards the tertiary sector
  • labour intensive: production which involves more human inputs relative to machinery
  • primary sector:extracting raw materials from the earth
  • secondary sector:converting raw materials into finished or semi-finished goods
  • tertiary sector: providing a wide variety of services

Key definitions

General vocabulary

  • industrialisation: a process where an economy moves away from primary sector activity towards a greater focus on manufacturing activity

Sectors of the economy

Sectors of the economy

Primary sector:extracting raw materials from the earth.

  • Involves agriculture, forestry, fishing and mining or quarrying raw materials.

Secondary sector: converting raw materials into finished or semi-finished goods.

  • Involves any form of manufacturing, construction or processing.
  • Turns inputs into goods that are ready for sale to an end consumer.

Tertiary sector: providing a wide variety of services.

  • Invoices selling goods to an end consumer or providing any form of service to individuals or businesses.
