Learning objectives

Learning objectives

In this lesson, students will learn to:

  • understand the main features of a social enterprise
  • compare different forms of ownership and select the most appropriate choices for different types of business.

Key definitions

Key definitions

  • charity: an organisation that provides assistance to people who are in need
  • consumer cooperative: a cooperative whose customers own it
  • cooperative: an organisation whose members own and control it; the members are often workers in the business or customers of the business
  • retail cooperative: a cooperative that is owned by retailers who work together to increase their buying power
  • social enterprise: a business that aims to improve human or environmental well-being
  • stakeholder: any individual or group with an interest in the operation of a business
  • worker cooperative: a cooperative that is owned by its members

Social enterprises

Social enterprises are sometimes called non-profit (or not-for-profit) organisations.

Usually, social enterprises:

  • have a particular non-financial objective that is at the core of the organisation’s existence
  • are controlled in the interests of that specific social mission
  • are funded by donations or running an activity that generates income

Key features of a social enterprise

  • Not controlled by any single, large shareholder
  • Primary objective is based on achieving some form of social fairness (equity)
  • Members elect managers to act in the best interests of workers, customers or third parties
  • Generate the majority of their income through trade or donations
  • Are accountable and transparent to any stakeholder
  • Return their ‘profits’ to members or good causes

ABC: Privatisation or nationalisation?

  • Problems associated with privatisation could include:
    1. The privatised company may withdraw important services from some citizens if they cannot afford to pay
    2. A private owner may significantly increase prices to make more profit

 

  • Problems with retaining nationalised status could include:
