Learning objectives

Learning objectives

In this lesson, students will learn to:

  • determine the main features of a public corporation
  • examine the main characteristics relating to the size of public corporations.

Key definitions

  • nationalisation: the transfer of ownership from the private to the public sector
  • natural monopoly: an industry where huge set-up costs mean it is only logical for one company to exist
  • privatisation: the transfer of public sector resources to the private sector
  • public corporations: business organisations owned by the government and run on behalf of the citizens of a country
  • public sector: business organisations owned by central or local government

Public corporations

Teaching session 2

Reasons for public ownership

Reasons for public ownership

Expand
  • Because private-sector companies will only supply if they can make a profit, some goods would be under-provided or not provided at all
Expand
  • Certain key industries (such as defence or communications) are seen as vital for a government to control in order to protect citizens
Expand
  • To protect the jobs of workers when private companies might be more influenced by profits
Expand
  • Because an industry is a natural monopoly and it is logical for just one company to exist and only the government is willing to provide the service
Expand
  • To ensure sufficient investment into a company or industry

Possible disadvantages of public ownership

  • The costs involved in providing services for free must be paid through taxes
  • High costs to taxpayer if public corporations make losses
  • Public corporations do not have an incentive to cut costs or be efficient
  • May be influenced by political rather than business considerations/objectives
