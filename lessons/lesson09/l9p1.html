In this lesson, students will learn to:

  • determine the main features of a public corporation
  • examine the main characteristics relating to the size of public corporations.

Students should already be familiar with:

  • the main forms of private ownership (see Lessons 5, 6 and 8)
  • the objectives of owners of public limited companies (see Lesson 7).
  • nationalisation: the transfer of ownership from the private to the public sector
  • natural monopoly: an industry where huge set-up costs mean it is only logical for one company to exist
  • privatisation: the transfer of public sector resources to the private sector
  • public corporations: business organisations owned by the government and run on behalf of the citizens of a country
  • public sector: business organisations owned by central or local government

No equipment is needed for this lesson.

  • Some students may be confused by the distinction between public limited companies and public corporations, which is understandable given the similarity in the names. This confusion can occur throughout the lesson but may well first appear during Teaching session 1. Tell students to focus on the ‘limited’ aspect of the plc name which tells them that this is a company owned by private individuals and is therefore owned by shareholders for their own personal gain.
