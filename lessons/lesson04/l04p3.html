Learning objectives

Learning objectives

In this lesson, students will learn to:

  • calculate percentage changes from figures given in a graph or table
  • calculate a number as a percentage of another number
  • calculate averages from figures from a graph or table.

Key definitions

Key definitions

  • cost of sales: costs such as raw materials and the wages of factory workers
  • expenses: costs not directly linked to output, such as rental costs or interest paid on loans
  • gross profit formula: gross profit = sales revenue – cost of sales
  • operating profit formula: operating profit = gross profit – expenses
  • labour productivity: output per worker in a given time period

Labour productivity is the average number of products each worker in the company produces:

Total output / No. of workers

Calculate the labour productivity of each company:

  • Company G employs 174 workers and produced 5,916 products last week. What was its labour productivity?
  • Company H employs 2,300 workers who produce an average of 71 products each year. How many products will the company produce in a year?
  • Company J has calculated its labour productivity as 16.75 units/worker. If it employs 1,190 workers how many products will be produced?

\(\frac{5916\;\mathrm{products}}{174\;\mathrm{workers}}=\;34\;\mathrm{units}\;\mathrm{per}\;\mathrm{worker}\)

2,300 workers × 71 products = 163,300 units will be produced in total during the year

1,190 workers × 16.75 units = 19,932 units in total

Question 2

2Lin’s company received revenue of £56,500 in 2022 and had costs of £41,290.

  1. Calculate Lin’s profit margin for 2022.
  2. Calculate Lin’s costs as a percentage of her sales for 2022.
  3. If Lin’s profit for 2021 was £9,834, calculate the percentage change in her profit in 2022.

Profit is £15,210 (£56,500 − £41,290) and so profit margin is 26.92% (\(\frac{15,210}{56,500}\times100\))

\(\frac{41,290}{56,500}\times100\;=\;\mathbf{73}\boldsymbol.\mathbf{08}\boldsymbol\%\)

Change in profit is £5,826 (£15,210 − £9,834) and so percentage increase is 62.08% (\(\frac{£5,826}{£9,834}\times100\))
