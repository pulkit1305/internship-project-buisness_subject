Lesson 7 | Overview Public Limited Companies

1.2.1 The main types of business ownership: limited companies (public).

1.2.2 Characteristics according to size:

  • concepts of risk, ownership and limited liability
  • ownership, control, sources of finance, use of profits and shareholders
  • appropriateness of different forms of ownership.

In this lesson, students will learn to:

  • understand the main business characteristics of public limited companies linked to size
  • examine the most appropriate form of ownership for different types of business.

Students should already be familiar with:

  • the difference between incorporated and unincorporated businesses (see Lesson 5)
  • many of the main features of limited liability companies (see Lesson 6), such aslimited liability, shareholders and directors.

Subject vocabulary

  • public limited company: a limited company in which shares can be sold to the general public and traded on a stock exchange
  • stock market flotation: when a private limited company first makes its shares available for the public to buy on the stock exchange (this means it becomes a public limited company)

General vocabulary

  • going public: the process of changing from a private limited company to a public limited company. This is sometimes called ‘flotation’

In this lesson, scissors will be required (minimum 12 pairs)

  • Some students may be confused by the fact that a private limited company is abbreviated to ltd whilst a public limited company is abbreviated to plc. This is because plc could easily stand for private limited company too. Therefore, this confusion is understandable but it is a really important distinction to recognise and remember. A useful mnemonic may be that ‘private’ contains a ‘t’ and so they should remember that ‘ltd’ (containing the ‘t’) is the correct abbreviation.
