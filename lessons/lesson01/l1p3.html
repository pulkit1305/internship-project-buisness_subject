Learning objectives

Learning objectives

In this lesson, students will learn to:

  • understand the different financial objectives of a business
  • answer different types of 1-mark exam questions.

Key definitions

Subject vocabulary

  • entrepreneur: a person who takes risks and sets up a business
  • financial security: when a business earns enough money to both survive and satisfy the needs of the owner
  • market share: proportion of sales in a total market that a business or product enjoys
  • objectives: goals or targets set by a business
  • profit: when revenue is greater than costs (total revenue – total costs)
  • revenue: money from the sale of goods and services
  • sales volume: the number of products a business sells in a particular period of time
  • sales value: the worth of all the goods a business sells in a particular period of time (selling price × quantity sold)
  • survival: when a business makes enough money to cover the costs it has to pay in the next few months

 

General vocabulary

  • command word: the verb in an exam question that explains what a student is required to do
  • financial: relating to money

Profit

  • Profit = Total revenue – Total costs (Profit = TR – TC)
  • Total revenue (sales revenue) = Selling price × Quantity sold (TR = SP × Q)

Questions

1If a business produces 100 cars each week and sells them for $10,000 each, how much total revenue will the business earn?

2How much profit will be left if it has total costs equalling $750,000/week?

Answers to Questions 1 and 2

1If a company produces 100 cars each week and sells them for $10,000 each, how much total revenue will it earn?

SP × Q = TR

So $10,000 × 100 = $1,000,000 of total revenue

2How much profit will be left if it has total costs equalling $750,000/week?

TR – TC = Profit

$1,000,000 – $750,000 = $250,000

Calculating market share

Example:

Company A sells 150 products each week. Total sales of that type of product within the market are 10,000 products per week. Its market share can be calculated by:


\(\frac{\mathrm{Company}\;\mathrm A'\mathrm s\;\mathrm{sales}}{\mathrm{Totale}\;\mathrm{market}\;\mathrm{sales}}\times100\)

So: \(\frac{150}{10,000}\times100=1.5\%\)


This means Company A receives 1.5% of all the sales in that particular market. Its market share is 1.5%.

Questions

Calculate these market shares.

1Between January and March 2022, Samsung sold 659,000 mobile phones in Australia out of a total of 1,806,000 million phones sold in the country.
What was Samsung’s market share (in terms of sales volume)?

2Tesco’s sales in the UK totalled £9.9 bn between March and May 2022.
If the total value of sales in its market was £36.8 bn, what was its market share (in value terms)?

Answer to Question 1

1Between January and March 2022, Samsung sold 659,000 mobile phones in Australia out of a total of 1,806,000 million phones sold in the country.
What was Samsung’s market share (in terms of sales volume)?

Answer

Formula: \(\frac{\mathrm{Samsung}'\mathrm s\;\mathrm{sales}}{\mathrm{Total}\;\mathrm{market}\;\mathrm{sales}}\times100\)

\(=\frac{659,000}{1,806,000}\times100\) (to turn into a percentage)

\(=36.5\%\)

Answer to Question 2

2Tesco’s sales in the UK totalled £9.9 bn between March and May 2022.
If the total value of sales in its market was £36.8 bn, what was its market share (in value terms)?

Answer

Formula: \(\frac{\mathrm{Tesco}'\mathrm s\;\mathrm{sales}\;(\mathrm{value})}{\mathrm{Total}\;\mathrm{market}\;\mathrm{sales}\;(\mathrm{value})}\times100\)

\(=\frac{9.9}{36.8}\times100\)

\(=26.9\%\)
