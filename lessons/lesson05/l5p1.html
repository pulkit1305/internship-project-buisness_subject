In this lesson, students will learn to understand the distinction between unincorporated and incorporated businesses.


As this is the beginning of a new topic, most students will not have any prior knowledge.

Subject vocabulary

  • deed of partnership: a legally binding document that states the formal rights of partners in a business
  • incorporated business: a business that has a separate legal identity from its owners
  • limited liability: when the business owner is only liable for the original amount of money invested in the business
  • partnership: a business owned by between 2 and 20 people
  • percentage change: change divided by the original value then multiplied by 100
  • sole trader: a business owned by a single person
  • unincorporated business:a business where there is no legal separation between the owner and the business
  • unlimited liability: when the owner of a business is personally liable for the debts of the business.

General vocabulary

  • legal structure: the way the law views how a business is set up, owned and run
