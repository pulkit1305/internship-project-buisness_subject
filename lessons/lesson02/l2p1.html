Lesson 2 | Overview Non-financial Aims and Objectives/Exam Skills 2

1.1.1
Businesses can have several objectives: non-financial aims and objectives – social objectives, personal satisfaction, challenge, independence and control.

In this lesson, students will learn to:

  • understand the different non-financial objectives that a business may have
  • distinguish between the different styles of 2- and 3-mark exam questions.

Students should already be familiar with:

  • the meaning of the term ‘objective’ within a business context (see Lesson 1)
  • the different types of financial objective that firms set for themselves (see Lesson 1)
  • how to answer 1-mark exam questions (see Lesson 1).
  • non-financial objectives: objectives that are not connected with making money
  • private sector: business organisations owned by individuals or groups of individuals
  • public sector: business organisations owned by central or local government
  • social objectives: objectives that improve human well-being

No equipment is needed for this lesson.

  • Some students may struggle to understand the difference between ‘outline’ and ‘explain’ questions. Emphasise that 2-mark ‘outline’ questions require a reference to a specific business (and will generally refer to the business in the question wording), whereas 3-mark ‘explain’ questions require a theoretical response (and therefore will not include a reference to a specific business within the question.) Some students may find it useful to use a mnemonic to remember this (for example, ‘Explain questions can be answered with a plain and simple theory’). Explain questions will need more detail as they require an initial point that is then extended twice using additional information.
  • Students may have difficulty distinguishing between different forms of non-financial objectives (e.g. ‘to prove to myself that I can achieve anything I set my mind to’ could be seen as fitting into both ‘Challenge’ and ‘Personal satisfaction’). Explain that these distinctions are subjective and that exam questions will never ask for a definitive categorisation: what is important is an understanding of the role of non-financial objectives.
