Learning objectives

Learning objectives

In this lesson, students will learn to:

  • understand the different non-financial objectives that a business may have
  • distinguish between the different styles of 2- and 3-mark exam questions.

Key definitions

Key definitions

  • non-financial objectives: objectives that are not connected with making money
  • private sector: business organisations owned by individuals or groups of individuals
  • public sector: business organisations owned by central or local government
  • social objectives: objectives that improve human well-being

Explain one reason that a business may decide to set non-financial objectives as well as financial ones. (3 marks)

Answer: To reflect the owner’s personal view about what is important to them. For example, they may set a non-financial objective to focus the business on a social cause they believe in and so will want to measure if they are having a positive impact by assessing how well they meet that objective.

Suggested answer to Question 4

Example answer to Question 4:

Setting an objective relating to an environmental goal may attract more customers. People who are concerned about social issues such as global warming may decide to buy from the business and may also inform their friends of the ‘green credentials’ of the firm, further increasing its sales.
