Learning objectives

Learning objectives

In this lesson, students will learn to:

  • recognise the impact that the internet has on business location
  • recognise the impact that government restrictions and trading blocs may have on business location.

Key definitions

Subject vocabulary

  • assisted areas: areas that are designated by a government as having economic problems and are targeted to receive support in a variety of forms
  • ‘footloose’ businesses: businesses who can operate anywhere rather than being permanently tied to any one location
  • subsidy: financial support given to a domestic producer to help compete with overseas businesses
  • tariff: a tax on imports to make them more expensive
  • trade bloc: a group of countries that join together and agree to reduce or eliminate the barriers to trade between themselves.

Key definitions

Key definitions: General vocabulary

  • amenities: something that makes a place comfortable or easy to live in
  • connectivity: the ability of computers and other electronic equipment to connect with other computers or programs
  • e-commerce or e-tailing: use of electronic systems to sell goods or services
  • infrastructure: systems or structures that a country needs in order to work properly e.g. roads or mobile phone masts.

The internet as an influence on business location

  • Goods can be sold online so reduced need for physical shops
  • Transport links vital for quick distribution of online orders
  • Businesses still need warehouses to distribute goods
  • Many services can be provided remotely
  • Cost savings available from smaller/no physical sites
  • Staff can collaborate online rather than in person

Government influences on business location

Government influences on business location

Legal controls:

  • Planning permission
  • Licences to carry out certain business activities
  • Restrictions on heavily polluting industries
  • Rules/laws to reduce or prevent congestion

Reasons why governments try to attract new businesses to a particular area:

  1. To boost employment in a particular area
  2. Because producing in their country creates tax revenues for the government
  3. To bring new investment/ideas/skilled workers to a country/area
  4. To create important new services for residents

Question: Think of as many ways as possible that a government could try to incentivise businesses to locate to a certain area or the country as a whole.

Government methods of attracting businesses to (re)locate in a certain location

  • Tax breaks (cuts)
  • Provide key services/infrastructure
  • Subsidise key activities
  • Relax work permit rules
  • Run training courses
  •  Promote the location directly to businesses

Challenge task

Task

Use the business you discussed at the beginning of the lesson.

Review the extent to which your business would be influenced by the government in our country. Prepare a brief report for your co-owners, which covers:

  • (Changes in) laws and regulations
  • Financial incentives and taxes that must be paid
  • How much it will cost in one location compared to another and why
  • Whether membership of a trade bloc influences location decisions
  • An overall decision about whether our government’s policies have a big enough impact to influence where and how your business operates or not.
